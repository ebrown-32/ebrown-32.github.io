Colors I


"The famed artist, author, print-maker, educator, and color theorist, Josef Albers compared good coloring to good cooking - they both demand repeated tasting (pg 11)."

Eiseman, L. (2017). The complete color harmony: Expert color information for professional results (Pantone edition). Rockport Publishers.

"Freedom"

My biggest takeaway is that much like cooking, we have the freedom to do just about anything with color. What we do with color will mean different things to different people, because color usage is very much psychological. Just like some food is repulsive to some and great to others, designs can be the same way. The key is to focus the freedom we have when choosing ingredients to best suit our audience.

So what?

There is a lot of depth to the seemingly simple concept of colors. The properties of light, wavelengths, characteristics of colors like shades and hues. This is all good stuff, but the key takeaway is to be intentional with the freedom that colors allow.
