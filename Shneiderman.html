Data is everywhere, evolving, and overwhelming. This paper dives into what a user needs to effectively consume data.

"Understanding"

Effective visualization requires an understanding of common user actions. This is my biggest takeaway from the paper:

  • Overview: Grasping the big picture.
  • Zoom: Focusing on specific data subsets.
  • Filter: Sifting out unimportant data.
  • Details-on-demand: Pulling up intricate information for specific items.
  • Relate: Spotting relationships in the data.
  • History: Keeping a log of user actions and data interactions.
  • Extract: Picking specific data from the visualization for other purposes.

So what?

Good products shouldn't just display data, they should promote meaningful interactions with said data. Data needs to be understood and consumed, not just seen.
