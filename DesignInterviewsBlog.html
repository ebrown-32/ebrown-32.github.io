Design Interviews


If your answer to the question "Are you ready for the design interview?" is no, welcome to the club! I'm no UI/UX designer, but today I'm going to provide you a short tutorial on how to ace a design interview with a few simple steps. I'm basing my tutorial off of this short video https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZtN3yHIWuRY&t=36s and adding some of my own wonderful commentary.

"Research"

This isn't exclusive to a design interview, but it's a crucial component to finding a good fit. Remember they aren't just interviewing you, you're interviewing them. Determining their needs provides you insight into what you'll actually be working on.

Prepare your Portfolio

A good portfolio is your money maker when it comes to a design interview. Make sure your best work is showcased, and spend a LOT of time making it.

Questions

Tell me about yourself. What inspires you? Strengths and weaknesses? Prepare and knock these out of the park. You'll also likely get the standard situational questions "Tell me about a time..." and stuff like that. Focus on the challenges you've overcome and walk them through the process of your thinking.
Ask questions! Interview them.

Relax

Honestly, a good portfolio will speak for itself. Make sure to prepare appropriately and be yourself. Good luck!
